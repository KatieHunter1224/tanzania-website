Kilimanjaro

Summary of the Lemosho Route (7 Nights, 8 Days) The Lemosho Route is widely regarded as one of the most scenic and successful trekking routes to the summit of Mount Kilimanjaro. The 8-day itinerary is designed to maximize acclimatization and summit success, offering a gradual ascent through diverse ecological zones—from lush rainforest to the arctic summit. Key Features: Begins on Kilimanjaro’s western side, typically at Londorossi Gate. Traverses six ecological zones, including rainforest, moorland, alpine desert, and arctic summit. Passes notable landmarks such as Shira Plateau, Lava Tower, Barranco Wall, and Barafu Camp. Offers excellent acclimatization with moderate daily altitude gains and an extra day for adaptation. Known for high summit success rates and stunning scenery, including unique flora like the giant Dendrosenecio plants. Typical 8-Day Itinerary: Day 1: Londorossi Gate to Mti Mkubwa (Big Tree) Camp (rainforest zone). Day 2: Mti Mkubwa to Shira 1 Camp (emerging from forest into moorland). Day 3: Shira 1 to Shira 2 Camp (across the Shira Plateau). Day 4: Shira 2 to Lava Tower, then descend to Barranco Camp (acclimatization day, passing through alpine desert). Day 5: Barranco Camp to Karanga Camp (climb the Barranco Wall, traverse valleys). Day 6: Karanga to Barafu Camp (alpine desert, final base before summit). Day 7: Summit attempt to Uhuru Peak (midnight start), then descend to Mweka Camp. Day 8: Final descent to Mweka Gate and transfer back to hotel for celebration.

  • Londorossi Gate – 2,100–2,300 m (6,890–7,546 ft)
  • Mti Mkubwa (Big Tree) Camp – 2,650–2,895 m (8,700–9,498 ft)
  • Shira 1 Camp – 3,500–3,536 m (11,500–11,600 ft)
  • Shira 2 Camp – 3,840–3,900 m (12,600–12,795 ft)
  • Barranco Camp – 3,900 m (12,795 ft)
  • Karanga Camp – 3,960–4,050 m (12,992–13,287 ft)
  • Barafu Camp – 4,650–4,670 m (15,255–15,321 ft)
  • Kosovo Camp (optional high camp) – 4,900 m (16,076 ft)
  • Uhuru Peak (Summit) – 5,895–5,896 m (19,341–19,340 ft)
  • Mweka Camp (descent) – 3,100 m (10,170 ft)
Distance: The total trekking distance is approximately 70 km (43 miles) over 8 days.
